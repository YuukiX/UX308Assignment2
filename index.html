Emergency Contact Keychain Device

March 2021 | UX202: Maker Lab II

As a group, we were tasked to create and design a speculative haptic tool that integrates media technology to help solve a problem within the city of Brantford. To regard the safety of walking outside in Brantford at night, we decide to design an Emergency Contact Keychain Device that would be given for free to Laurier students. Our device includes a flashlight, GPS tracker, and an emergency button that would contact your location to the police immediately. In addition, the keychain would be linked to the LORIS account of the specific student, and their profile can be accessed through the Laurier’s Safety Hawk app. The app provides further details and displays areas on the map that may be dangerous.








COVID-19 Occupancy Tracker

April 2021 | UX202: Maker Lab II

As a group, we were tasked to design a speculative product that had a electronic component that connects to the internet and involves somatic experiences to solve a problem of our choice. Our team came up with the COVID-19 Occupancy Tracker that counts the amount of customers entering/exiting stores during the pandemic. Our design uses motion sensors that connects to automatic doors along with a tv monitor that would display the current count outside of the store. We also designed an optional app for customers to download that would showcase the current count with live updates and specific COVID protocols from the store the user has selected.








Laurier Registration Guide

UX100: Design Foundations I

Together as Team MOSEEY, we were tasked by Wilfrid Laurier University to ideate a solution to provide a seamless registration process for incoming and mature students. Our solution involves redesigning the current registration guide by reducing text heavy content, adding more visuals/graphics, and making sure students can find non-restricted electives relative to their program.
